The IT Act enables the use of digital signatures and electronic documents in legal proceedings. It defines cybercrime and provides for penalties and punishment for offenses such as hacking, phishing, and spreading of viruses. The act provides for the establishment of a Cyber Appellate Tribunal to deal with appeals against orders passed by the Adjudicating Officer. It recognizes the role of intermediaries such as internet service providers and social media platforms and provides them with immunity from legal action for content posted by third parties. It empowers the government to intercept and monitor electronic communication in certain cases for national security purposes. The IT Act also provides for the protection of sensitive personal information and data by imposing penalties for unauthorized access and disclosure of such information.
